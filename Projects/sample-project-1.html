Overview

This project documents the complete design and construction of a fully off-grid cabin, from concept to completion. The goal was to create a sustainable, comfortable living space powered by renewable energy, using reclaimed materials wherever possible.

Design and Planning

The design process began with rough sketches and digital modeling to determine the optimal layout and orientation for solar exposure. Structural plans were adjusted to maximize insulation efficiency and reduce long-term maintenance needs.

Materials were sourced locally, with a focus on reusing lumber and fixtures to reduce cost and environmental impact.

Construction and Systems

Construction was completed over several months, featuring a raised foundation, passive cooling techniques, and a hybrid solar battery setup for power. The cabin includes a rainwater collection system and composting toilet, allowing it to operate fully off-grid.

Results and Reflections

The completed cabin demonstrates the viability of small-scale, sustainable living with minimal footprint. The process offered valuable insights into real-world project management, material selection, and renewable system integration.
